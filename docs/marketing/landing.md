# Landing page (`/`)

## Job of this page

1. **Explain FESTR in one breath** — first emergency support: reporting, resources, education, responder alignment (phased).
2. **Earn trust** — who built it, why it exists, no exaggerated claims.
3. **Drive one primary action** — e.g. “Explore the app” or “Join the pilot” (single dominant button).

Visitors decide in **~10 seconds** whether to scroll or leave. The fold must answer: **what / for whom / why now**.

---

## Proposed section order

### 1. Hero

**Layout intent:** One strong headline, one supporting line, one primary CTA, optional secondary link (text style). Background: subtle, brand-aligned (not busy stock photo collage).

**Headline direction (pick one lane during implementation):**

- *Outcome-led:* “Turn bystanders into a lifeline.”
- *Product-led:* “Emergency reporting, resources, and education — in one place.”

**Subhead (1–2 sentences):** FESTR helps people who witness emergencies share **location-tagged** information, discover **life-saving resources** (e.g. AEDs), and learn **basic emergency steps** — with a path for official coordination as partnerships grow.

**Primary CTA:** Verb + destination clarity — e.g. “Open FESTR” → app entry, or “Request pilot access” → contact anchor/form.

**Secondary:** “How it works” → anchor to section below.

**Trust micro-line (optional, one line):** e.g. “Built with frontline emergency experience.” (No fake metrics.)

---

### 2. Problem / insight (short)

**Purpose:** Frame the gap without lecturing.

**Content blocks:**

- Pre-hospital and coordination gaps in many regions; bystanders often **unsure** or only **spectating**.
- FESTR’s angle: **structured** reporting and resource visibility, plus education — not replacing emergency services, **supporting** them.

**Visual:** Simple two-column or stacked facts; avoid long paragraphs.

---

### 3. What FESTR does (pillars)

Three or four **cards** (not more — scannable):

| Pillar | User-facing label | One line |
|--------|-------------------|----------|
| Report | Real-time incident reporting | Location, context, optional media — routed for response workflows. |
| Map | Resource awareness | AEDs and critical points on a map (crowdsourced, verification over time). |
| Learn | Emergency education | Short, practical guidance for laypeople. |
| Coordinate (phase label) | Responder alignment | Dashboards and partnerships as the system matures. |

Use a small “Phase” or “Coming soon” badge only where honest — do not promise live police dispatch if not shipped.

---

### 4. Differentiators (tight, not a feature list)

**Bullets (max 4):**

- Designed for **real-world constraints** (mobile web, variable connectivity).
- **Silent / discreet help** concept for situations where voice calls aren’t safe — position carefully; link to privacy/safety messaging.
- **Education** bundled with reporting — not only a map app.
- **Credibility:** grounded in emergency nursing and pre-hospital coordination (see About).

---

### 5. Who it’s for

Two rows or tabs: **Public** vs **Organizations** (ambulance, hospitals, NGOs). One sentence each — who benefits and how.

---

### 6. Call to action (repeat)

Repeat primary CTA + optional email capture only if you have a real list and privacy copy.

---

### 7. Footer strip

Link to About, Contact, Privacy. Optional: “Built in [region]” if you want locality without clutter.

## SEO

| Field | Guidance |
|-------|----------|
| **Title** | `FESTR — First Emergency Support & Triage Response` (or shorter if brand-only). |
| **Meta description** | ~155 chars: reporting + resources + education + bystanders + low-resource / coordination angle. |

---

## Non-goals on v1 landing

- Live incident feed from production data.
- Full comparison table vs other apps (save for a blog or one-pager PDF).
- Auto-playing video or heavy animation that hurts LCP.
