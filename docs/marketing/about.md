# About page (`/about`)

## Job of this page

Turn **curiosity into confidence**: why FESTR exists, what principles guide it, and **who stands behind it**. This is the credibility page — not a duplicate of the landing hero.

---

## Proposed section order

### 1. Page intro

**H1:** “About FESTR” or “Why FESTR exists”

**Lead paragraph (3–4 sentences):** State the mission in plain language: improving how communities connect during emergencies through **timely information**, **resource visibility**, and **education**, with infrastructure that can grow toward deeper responder integration.

Avoid repeating the full landing headline verbatim — complement it.

---

### 2. The problem (framed responsibly)

Short sections with clear headings:

- **Delays and fragmentation** in pre-hospital and coordination pathways in many settings.
- **Information gaps** — where AEDs and basic resources are, what to do first.
- **Behavioral reality** — people may film or share before they help; the product’s job is to **channel action**, not moralize.

Tone: factual, compassionate, no sensational imagery.

---

### 3. Principles (how FESTR is built)

Bullet list of **design and ethics principles** (implementation-agnostic):

- **Speed under stress** — minimal steps for reporting.
- **Honesty about phase** — what works today vs what requires partners/policy.
- **Privacy-aware** especially for sensitive incidents and location.
- **Accessibility** — mobile-first, readable type, clear language.

---

### 4. What FESTR is / isn’t

| FESTR is | FESTR is not (at launch) |
|----------|---------------------------|
| A channel to share structured incident information and location | A replacement for national emergency numbers |
| A map and education layer for public benefit | A guarantee of dispatch or response time |
| A foundation for partnerships | A complete government integration by default |

Adjust rows as features ship — keep this table updated.

---

### 5. Founder / team (humanize)

**Purpose:** Connect faces and roles to trust.

Suggested structure:

- **Name and role** (e.g. Emergency nurse; pre-hospital coordination; training initiatives).
- **2–3 paragraphs** on experience: what you’ve seen on the ground, why technology matters **here**, what success looks like for communities.
- **Optional:** logo or photo of affiliated organization **only if permitted** and high quality.

Do not invent credentials; align with real CV and public profiles.

---

### 6. Roadmap narrative (high level)

Three phases in **plain language** (not a Gantt chart on this page):

1. **Now / next** — core flows: report, map, learn; auth and partnerships as planned.
2. **Partner integration** — NGOs, private ambulance, selected institutions.
3. **Deeper integration** — broader official channels where policy allows.

Link to technical roadmap only if you publish one elsewhere.

---

### 7. Closing CTA

Single line + button: “Try FESTR” / “Contact us” — match landing primary action.
