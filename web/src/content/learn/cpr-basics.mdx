# CPR basics (lay responder)

This is **education only**, not a substitute for certified training or dispatcher instructions. In an emergency, **call your local emergency number** first (or have someone else call) while you begin help.

## Check responsiveness and breathing

1. Tap the shoulders and shout.
2. If the person is unresponsive and **not breathing normally** (or only gasping), treat it as cardiac arrest in a simplified lay model.

## Chest compressions

- Place the heel of one hand on the **center of the chest** (lower half of sternum).
- Place your other hand on top, interlace fingers, **arms straight**, shoulders over hands.
- Push **hard and fast** — about **5–6 cm (2–2.4 in)** depth for adults, at roughly **100–120 compressions per minute**.
- Allow **full recoil** between compressions (let the chest come all the way up).
- **Minimize pauses** — switch compressors every 2 minutes if another rescuer is available.

## Rescue breaths (if trained and willing)

If you are trained and there is no barrier concern, give **30 compressions then 2 breaths**. If not trained or unwilling to give breaths, **compression-only CPR** is acceptable in many protocols until an AED or advanced help arrives.

## AED

- Turn it on and **follow the voice prompts**.
- Expose the chest, attach pads as shown, and **stand clear** during analysis and shock delivery.

## Aftercare

If the person responds, place them in a **recovery position** only if spinal injury is not suspected and they are breathing normally — otherwise keep monitoring and follow dispatcher guidance.
