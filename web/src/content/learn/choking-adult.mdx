# Choking - Overview

A choking person's airway may be completely or partially blocked. A complete blockage is an urgent medical emergency. A partial obstruction can quickly become life threatening if the person loses the ability to breathe in and out sufficiently. Without oxygen, permanent brain damage can occur in as little as 4 minutes. Rapid first aid for choking can save a life.

**The universal distress signal for choking is grabbing the throat with one or both hands.**

**DO NOT perform first aid** if the person is coughing forcefully and able to speak - **a strong cough can dislodge the object on its own**.

## What To DO
Ask the person:

"Are you choking?"

"Can you speak?"

If someone can **speak, cough forcefully, or breathe**, encourage them to keep coughing and stay with them.

## Severe obstruction

If the person **cannot speak, or only nods, has weak or silent cough, or shows signs of severe distress**:

1. **Call emergency services** (or send someone to call).
2. **Back blows**: stand slightly to the side and behind, support the chest with one hand, deliver up to **5 firm blows** between the shoulder blades with the heel of your other hand.
3. If not cleared, **abdominal thrusts (Heimlich Maneuver)** for responsive adults **if you are trained** — stand behind, hands just above the navel, quick inward-upward thrusts.

## If the person becomes unresponsive

- **Lower them carefully** to the ground, **open the airway**, and begin **CPR** as trained — check the mouth for a visible object before breaths and remove it if reachable.
